3.1 Evaluation Methodology

{Describe what a HE is and how it was done. Write between 300 to 500 of your own words. Explain the method, do not just list the heuristics. Replace my sample text below with your own.}

A heuristic evaluation is a usability inspection method used to look for usability errors in the ui (user interface) design. This inspection method was first described by Nielsen and Molich in 1990 [Nie1990]. This method involves having a small set of evaluators examine the interface and judge its compliance with recognized usability principles, known as heuristics. The small set of evaluators usually consist of usability specialists. The primary goal of this evaluation is to find the usability flaws so that they can be addressed as part of an iterative design process, enhancing the overall user experience. First there were nine heuristics defined in Nielsen and Molich [1990] then some got renamed and the tenth heuristic was added in Nielsen [Nie1994]. These heuristics and their applications are further detailed in Jakob Nielsen's guide on the subject, "Heuristic Evaluation” [Nie2022], he also writes on the importance of these ratings in his article, "10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design," where he emphasizes the practical impact of heuristic evaluation on interface usability [Nie2024]. For this heuristic evaluation of the website Bershka.com, the “Andrews General Usability Heuristics” were used. First each evaluator independently reviews the user interface, navigating through it as users would do under normal usage conditions. During this phase, evaluators note any deviations from these heuristics, which represent potential usability issues. Besides taking notes the evaluators also record the evaluation. Afterwards the video is cut into short 10 to 20 second clips. The notes taken by the evaluator contain a short summary of the accruing problem. After every evaluator has independently reviewed the user interface, they get together to rate each positive and negative finding. Every evaluator can rate every negative point form 0 (not a problem) to 4 (catastrophic problem) and every positive point also from 0 (not a positive) to 4 (Extremely Positive). After discussing which are the worst problems there is an executive summary of the main results written from the heuristic evaluators. This executive summary is aimed to the higher management of the tested product. The final report, which is including the executive summary and detailed findings, empowers the design team to make informed decisions about addressing and fixing usability issues.

The fashion style of the website is shifted more towards trendy clothes and therefore seems to have a younger audience in mind. With this the following user categories might be concluded

  • Teens
    • Characteristic: Consumes social media and knows which styles are currently hip. May want to have a similar look then their peers with individual accents.
    • Goal: Find something to wear that is currently trendy, but with a special twist in order to stand out. May also try to find a bargain in the sale section.
    • Task: Scout the website for trendy pieces and also check the sale section.
  • Young adults
    • Characteristic: Wants to have urban trendy fashion. Is probably employed and can afford to look for more expensive pieces. Looks for a specific piece of clothing.
    • Goal: Find something specific, like a shirt.
    • Task: Scout the website for the specific piece of clothing, check also the sale section.
  • Parents
    • Characteristic: Looks for something to wear for their kids. May not be in touch with current trends and goes by suggestions on the website.
    • Goal: Find something as a present for their child.
    • Task: Go through the gender specific section and look for something colorful. Mostly inspired by big pictures.
